# F-2.2.12 - Calibration Management

## Requirement ID
F-2.2.12

## Function Name
Calibration Management

## Function Description
Manage calibration schedules and records for measurement and test equipment including calibration cycle management, calibration certificate management with document attachment, overdue alerts, and measurement uncertainty tracking. Critical for quality management systems (ISO 9001), FDA 21 CFR Part 820 regulatory compliance, and GAMP 5 pharmaceutical manufacturing.

## Business Purpose
Ensure all measurement and test equipment produces accurate, reliable results by maintaining proper calibration schedules and records. Supports quality management systems, regulatory compliance, and manufacturing excellence by providing documented evidence of equipment fitness for use. Enables traceability and accountability in measurement-dependent processes.

## Core Business Rules

1. **Calibration Requirement**: Only equipment designated as requiring calibration appears in calibration management.

2. **Qualified Technician**: Only technicians with valid certifications for the specific equipment type can perform calibrations.

3. **Valid Procedure**: Each calibration must reference an approved calibration procedure for the equipment type.

4. **As-Found Recording**: Before any adjustment, record the "as-found" (current) measurement values.

5. **As-Left Recording**: After adjustment (if needed), record the "as-left" (post-adjustment) measurement values.

6. **Tolerance Evaluation**: Both as-found and as-left values must be within the defined tolerance limits.

7. **Pass/Fail Determination**: Calibration result is PASS if within tolerance, FAIL if outside tolerance.

8. **Quarantine on Failure**: Equipment that fails calibration is quarantined until successfully recalibrated.

9. **Auto-Scheduling**: Upon successful calibration, automatically calculate and set the next calibration due date.

10. **Overdue Alert**: Generate alerts when equipment approaches or exceeds its calibration due date.

11. **Certificate Attachment**: Successful calibrations should have a certificate document attached as evidence.

12. **Trend Analysis**: Track calibration history to identify drift patterns and potentially extend intervals for consistently passing equipment.

## Equipment States

| State | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| CALIBRATED | Equipment has valid calibration within due date |
| CALIBRATION_DUE | Equipment approaching calibration due date |
| OVERDUE | Equipment past calibration due date |
| QUARANTINE | Equipment failed calibration, not for use |

## Calibration States

| State | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| SCHEDULED | Calibration planned but not yet performed |
| IN_PROGRESS | Calibration currently being performed |
| PASSED | Calibration completed successfully |
| FAILED | Calibration did not meet tolerance requirements |
| CANCELLED | Calibration cancelled |

## Compliance Notes

**GAMP 5 / FDA 21 CFR Part 820 (Pharma/Medical Devices):**
- Calibration records constitute electronic records requiring 21 CFR Part 11 compliance
- Electronic signatures required for calibration completion
- Calibration management for critical instruments mandatory

**ISO 9001:2015:**
- Clause 7.1.5 requires documented calibration evidence
- Quality records demonstrate equipment fitness for purpose

**ISO 17025:**
- Laboratory calibration certificates require accreditation body recognition
- Measurement uncertainty must be quantified

**OSHA:**
- Air monitoring equipment calibration (29 CFR 1910.1450)
- Respiratory protection meters calibration (29 CFR 1910.134)

**EPA:**
- Continuous Emission Monitoring Systems (CEMS) require calibration records (40 CFR Part 60)
- Emission monitoring calibration (40 CFR Part 63)

## Validation Rules (Plain Language)

1. **Equipment Requires Calibration**: The equipment must be designated as requiring calibration.

2. **Calibration Due**: The current date must be on or past the scheduled calibration date.

3. **Technician Qualified**: The technician must hold a valid certification for the equipment type being calibrated.

4. **Procedure Exists**: An approved calibration procedure must exist for the equipment type.

5. **As-Found Within Tolerance**: Measurement before adjustment must not exceed tolerance limits.

6. **As-Left Within Tolerance**: Measurement after adjustment must not exceed tolerance limits.

7. **Certificate for Pass**: A pass result should have a calibration certificate attached.

## Edge Cases

| Scenario | Expected Behavior |
|----------|-------------------|
| Equipment failed calibration | Quarantine equipment, generate exception, initiate recalibration |
| Technician lacks required certification | Reject calibration attempt |
| Calibration data lost | Restore from last known good backup |
| Procedure updated during calibration | Complete using the current active procedure version |
| External lab performed calibration | Record external certificate via manual entry and document upload |
| Three consecutive passes | Consider extending calibration interval if trend analysis permits |

## Calibration Process Flow

1. **Calibration Due** → Retrieve calibration procedure
2. **Perform Calibration** → Record as-found values
3. **Evaluate As-Found** → If out of tolerance, perform adjustment
4. **Record As-Left Values** → Calculate measurement uncertainty
5. **Evaluate As-Left** → Pass if within tolerance
6. **On Pass** → Update next calibration date, attach certificate
7. **On Fail** → Generate exception, quarantine equipment

## Next Date Calculation

Next calibration date = Current calibration date + Calibration interval (days or months)
