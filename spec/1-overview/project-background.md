# Project Background

## Purpose

This asset management system is designed for multinational manufacturing corporations to track, manage, and optimize their physical assets across global operations. The system supports multiple languages, currencies, and compliance requirements for Western markets (US/EU) with particular focus on manufacturing enterprises.

## Target Market

The system is specifically designed for:
- Multinational manufacturing corporations
- Companies with multiple factories across different regions
- Enterprise groups with multiple subsidiary companies
- Organizations requiring strict regulatory compliance (SOX, GDPR, ISO 55000)

## Key Challenges Addressed

1. **Fragmented Asset Data**: Many organizations struggle with scattered asset information across different systems and locations

2. **Limited Visibility**: Lack of real-time visibility into asset status, location, and utilization across the enterprise

3. **Compliance Complexity**: Managing compliance with multiple regulatory frameworks including SOX, GDPR, OSHA, EPA, and international accounting standards

4. **Maintenance Inefficiency**: Reactive rather than proactive maintenance leading to unexpected downtime and high repair costs

5. **Multi-Entity Management**: Difficulty managing assets across multiple legal entities with different accounting standards and reporting requirements

## System Scope

The system covers:
- All types of physical assets (equipment, machinery, vehicles, furniture, IT hardware)
- Assets across all stages of the lifecycle (procurement to disposal)
- Multiple organizational units and legal entities
- Both centralized and distributed operational models
- 6-level organizational hierarchy: Group → Company → Division → Plant → Department → Cost Center

## Regulatory Framework

| Region | Key Regulations |
|--------|-----------------|
| Global | ISO 55000 (Asset Management) |
| US | SOX, US GAAP, OSHA, EPA |
| EU | IFRS, GDPR, WEEE Directive |
