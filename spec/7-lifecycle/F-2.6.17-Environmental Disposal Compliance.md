# F-2.6.17 - Environmental Disposal Compliance

**Requirement ID:** F-2.6.17  
**Module:** Asset Lifecycle Management  
**Priority:** High

---

## Requirement Description

Ensure asset disposal complies with environmental regulations, especially compliance management for hazardous substances, electronic waste, and recycling requirements.

---

## Business Rules

1. WEEE electronics require WEEE-certified processor and recycling certificate
2. Hazardous waste requires RCRA manifest (EPA Form 8700-22) from EPA-certified handler
3. RoHS restricted substances (Lead, Mercury, Cadmium, Cr6+, PBB, PBDE) require substance declaration
4. SVHC (Substances of Very High Concern) >0.1% triggers ECHA notification
5. Regulation database must be current (within 90 days)
6. Certified processor must be contracted for asset type
7. Universal waste rules apply to batteries, lamps, electronics in US

## Validation Rules

1. Asset may require environmental review if contains hazardous materials
2. Regulation database must be updated within 90 days
3. Certified processor must be available for asset type
4. Recycling certificate must be valid
5. EPA manifest required for hazardous waste disposal

---

## Compliance Requirements

- WEEE: Producer registration in EU member states; crossed-out wheelie bin marking; annual reporting; recycling certificates
- EPA/RCRA: EPA handler ID; Form 8700-22 manifest; 3-year record retention; exception reporting within 35 days
- RoHS: Supplier substance declaration; 10-year record retention; max concentration 0.1% (0.01% for Cadmium)
- REACH: SVHC article-level inventory; notification if >0.1% SVHC; ECHA notification if >1 tonne/year
- ISO 55000/14001: Environmental responsibility and EMS integration
