# F-2.8.8 - Centralized Procurement and Company Allocation

## Function ID
F-2.8.8

## Function Name
Group Centralized Procurement

## Function Description
Support group centralized procurement of assets, then allocation to subsidiaries, including procurement negotiation, contract signing, asset receiving, internal allocation, and cost sharing.

## Business Rules
1. Centralized procurement process: Demand aggregation → Supplier selection → Negotiation → Contract signing → Procurement execution
2. Allocation mechanism: Allocate assets to subsidiaries based on demand, budget, and usage plan
3. Cost sharing: Allocate procurement costs to subsidiaries by allocation ratio, supporting multiple allocation methods
4. Internal settlement: Inter-subsidiary cost settlement, internal receivable/payable reconciliation
5. Tax optimization: Leverage centralized procurement scale for tax optimization
6. Supplier management: Centralized supplier evaluation, performance management, relationship management
7. Compliance requirements: Meet procurement regulations of different countries/regions (bidding, antitrust, etc.)
8. Performance tracking: Centralized procurement cost savings, quality improvement, delivery time performance indicators

## Preconditions
- Each subsidiary procurement demand aggregatable
- Centralized procurement strategy formulated
- Allocation rules defined
- Cost allocation method determined

## Postconditions
- Centralized procurement contract signed
- Assets allocated to subsidiaries
- Cost sharing completed
- Performance tracking started

## Input Data
1. Each subsidiary procurement demand
2. Supplier selection criteria
3. Negotiation strategy and bottom line
4. Allocation rules and ratios
5. Cost allocation method
6. Internal settlement rules
7. Tax optimization strategy
8. Performance indicator definition

## Output Data
1. Centralized procurement plan
2. Procurement contract
3. Asset allocation plan
4. Cost allocation calculation table
5. Internal settlement records
6. Tax optimization report
7. Supplier performance evaluation
8. Centralized procurement performance report

## Validation Rules
1. Procurement demand must be reasonable (avoid over-procurement)
2. Supplier selection must be fair and compliant
3. Allocation must be based on reasonable rules
4. Cost allocation must be fair and accurate
5. Tax optimization must be legal

## Error Handling
1. Unreasonable demand: Communicate with subsidiaries to adjust demand
2. Supplier problem: Alternative supplier or re-tender
3. Allocation dispute: Group coordinates to resolve
4. Cost allocation dispute: Finance department arbitration

## Priority
High
