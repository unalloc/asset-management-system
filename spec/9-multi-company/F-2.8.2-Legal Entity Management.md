# F-2.8.2 - Legal Entity Management

## Function ID
F-2.8.2

## Function Name
Legal Entity Management

## Function Description
Manage legal entities (companies) within the group, including company registration information, legal status, tax information, and independent book settings.

## Business Rules
1. Company types: Wholly-owned subsidiary, controlling subsidiary, minority-owned company, joint venture, branch office
2. Registration information: Company name, registered address, registration number, registered capital, legal representative
3. Legal status: Business status (normal, closed, liquidation, deregistered), business term
4. Tax information: Taxpayer identification number, tax registration location, applicable tax types and rates
5. Independent books: Each legal entity has independent accounting books, supporting different accounting standards
6. Company relationships: Ownership relationships (shareholding ratio, investment date), control relationships
7. Compliance requirements: Meet corporate legal requirements of different countries/regions
8. Audit tracking: Company information change audit tracking

## Preconditions
- Legal entity legal information available
- Accounting standards defined
- User has "Legal Entity Management" permission
- Tax rules database available

## Postconditions
- Legal entity record created
- Independent book settings
- Tax configuration completed
- Ownership relationship record

## Input Data
1. Company code (unique identifier)
2. Company name (legal name, common name)
3. Company type
4. Registration information (address, registration number, capital, legal representative)
5. Legal status
6. Tax information
7. Accounting standards (China GAAP, International GAAP, etc.)
8. Ownership relationship data

## Output Data
1. Legal entity record
2. Independent book configuration
3. Tax configuration
4. Ownership structure chart
5. Compliance check report
6. Audit tracking record

## Validation Rules
1. Company code must be unique
2. Registration information must be complete (according to company type)
3. Tax information must comply with local regulations
4. Sum of ownership ratios must be reasonable (e.g., subsidiary ownership sums ≤100%)
5. Accounting standards must be supported

## Error Handling
1. Company already exists: Prompt duplication and suggest merge or modify code
2. Invalid tax information: Prompt tax verification failure
3. Accounting standard not supported: Prompt to select supported standard or request extension
4. Ownership relationship contradiction: Detect ownership circular or ratio anomaly

## Priority
High
