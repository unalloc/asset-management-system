# F-2.8.11 - Inter-company Cost Allocation

## Function ID
F-2.8.11

## Function Name
Inter-company Cost Allocation

## Function Description
Manage inter-company cost allocation within the group, including shared service costs, centralized procurement costs, and group management fees, supporting fair and reasonable cost sharing and settlement.

## Business Rules
1. Cost types: Shared service costs (IT, HR, Finance), centralized procurement costs, group management fees, shared facility costs
2. Allocation basis: Number of employees, revenue proportion, asset value, usage volume, floor space
3. Allocation methods: Direct allocation, step allocation, reciprocal allocation
4. Settlement mechanism: Internal settlement invoice, internal receivable/payable account, internal payment
5. Dispute handling: Cost allocation dispute appeal, arbitration, adjustment
6. Tax considerations: Tax compliance of cost allocation (transfer pricing, VAT)
7. Reporting analysis: Cost allocation report, allocation fairness analysis, cost savings analysis
8. Budget integration: Cost allocation integrated with budget preparation and budget control

## Preconditions
- Cost data to be allocated available
- Allocation basis data accurate
- Allocation method determined
- Settlement process defined

## Postconditions
- Cost allocation calculation completed
- Internal settlement invoices generated
- Dispute handling records saved
- Reports generated

## Input Data
1. Cost type and amount
2. Beneficiary company list
3. Allocation basis data
4. Allocation method parameters
5. Settlement rules
6. Dispute handling process
7. Tax compliance rules
8. Reporting requirements

## Output Data
1. Cost allocation calculation table
2. Internal settlement invoice
3. Internal receivable/payable entries
4. Dispute handling records
5. Tax compliance report
6. Cost allocation report
7. Fairness analysis
8. Budget integration data

## Validation Rules
1. Cost data must be accurate
2. Allocation basis must be fair and reasonable
3. Allocation method must be appropriate
4. Settlement must be timely
5. Tax must be compliant

## Error Handling
1. Basis data missing: Use alternative basis or negotiate to determine
2. Allocation unfair: Adjust allocation method or basis
3. Settlement failed: Investigate reason and resettle
4. Tax risk: Tax department reviews and adjusts

## Priority
High
